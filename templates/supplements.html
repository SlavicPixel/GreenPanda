When we think of supplements, the first thought that comes to mind are probably protein powders, multivitamin capsules and similar types of supplements.

Today, we'll rather look at four whole foods which you can treat as your daily supplements and one which is not a whole food, but also very important.

Flax Seed

Flax seed is very rich in Omega-3 fatty acids. Omega-3 fatty acids modulate a lot of processes in a human body, including the brain, visual development, inflammatory reactions, cancer growth and many others. Make sure to get grounded flax seeds or grind them yourself because otherwise, your body won't be able to absorb it.

Brazil Nut

Brazil nuts are a great source of selenium. Selenium is very important for our glutathione which plays important roles in antioxidant defense. Be careful, having too much selenium every day can be toxic for our body. Eating two Brazil nuts or even just one a day is more than enough to hit your selenium RDI (Reference Daily Intake).

Turmeric

The active component in turmeric we'll be looking at is curcumin. Curcumin is a powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant. It will easily help you with muscle inflammation after a hard exercise or other physical activities. It's important to note that you should consume turmeric with black pepper since it greatly helps our bodies to absorb curcumin.

Seaweed

Seaweed is a great source of a lot of minerals, but the important one is iodine. Iodine is essential for our thyroid and to keep its hormones in balance. Dulse seaweed is the best option since it has a higher amount of iodine compared to some other seaweeds.

Vitamin B12

We can't talk about supplements without mentioning vitamin B12. B12 has a very important role in function and development of brain and nerve cells. It is also needed to form red blood cells and DNA. B12 may also improve mood and symptoms of depression.
